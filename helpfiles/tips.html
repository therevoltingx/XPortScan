Tips


Port Scanning is more of an art.
With time you learn to be stealthy. But you will learn that with time and research, I am not here to teach you that.

Speed:
XportScan uses a technique known as threading to make port scanning as fast as possible.
(Windows note: Right now I have yet to seen a port scanner that is as fast or faster then XportScan.)
To increase speed you can select to only scan ports from portlist.txt and increase the number of threads.

Threads (Warning):
If you select too many threads the XportScan may crash, so I recommend just sticking to the default value but it all depends on how fast and powerfull your system is.
For example, under Windows, if you are scanning yourself, you may want to increase the number of threads, around 500.
But, if you are scanning yourself under Linux, disable threads, this increases speed dramatically.
